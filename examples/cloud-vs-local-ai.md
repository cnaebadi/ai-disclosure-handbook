# Cloud AI vs Local AI — A Realistic Tradeoff Model

The debate between cloud AI and local AI is often oversimplified.

In reality, neither is inherently “safe” or “unsafe”.

Each introduces different constraints and risks.

---

# Cloud AI Systems

Cloud-based AI systems run on external infrastructure.

## Advantages

* Access to large-scale models
* Continuous updates
* High performance hardware
* Better general reasoning capabilities
* Easier usability

---

## Risks

* Data leaves your device
* Dependence on third-party policies
* Potential logging and retention policies
* Legal jurisdiction concerns
* Long-term data governance uncertainty

---

## Important Clarification

Cloud AI is not automatically insecure.

It is simply externally governed.

---

# Local AI Systems

Local AI runs on user-owned hardware.

## Advantages

* Data remains on-device
* Higher control over storage
* Reduced external dependency
* Better sovereignty over workflows

---

## Risks

* Device compromise (malware, theft)
* Misconfiguration
* Lack of security updates
* Lower model performance (in many cases)
* User responsibility for security increases

---

## Important Clarification

Local AI is not automatically private.

It is simply locally governed.

---

# The False Dichotomy

Many discussions frame this as:

> Cloud = unsafe
> Local = safe

This is incorrect.

A better model is:

> Cloud = externally managed risk
> Local = internally managed risk

---

# The Real Question

The important question is not:

“Where does the model run?”

It is:

> “Who has control over the data lifecycle and inference outcomes?”

---

# Key Insight

Both cloud and local AI shift the burden of privacy.

They do not remove it.

They relocate it.
